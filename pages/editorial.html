EDITORIAL

1

News

Black queer students often face exclusion, highlighting the urgent need for inclusive environments that reflect their intersectional identities. Additionally, disproportionate violence against Black transgender women shows the need for targeted action, with activists calling for systemic change and greater support for Black trans-led initiatives.

2

Revisiting

Two decades after Eduardo Bonilla-Silva’s foundational text, this series of interviews with college students rethinks the ways that color-blind racism has both evolved and remained consistent with the times.

3

Creative

Tayo and Ryry are innocent childhood friends, but all changes the moment they are enrolled in school. This short story explores how divisions between us are not drawn on our skin, but filed away in ledgers and written into history.

4

Link it to art

Manet’s world-famous Olympia has long been associated with shock. Many aspects of the painting have been discussed in isolation, but rarely brought into relation with one another through the lens of intersectionality.

A concise editorial overview of the four sections in this issue.

About

Intersectionality is the interconnected nature of social categorizations such as race, class, and gender as they apply to a given individual or group, regarded as creating overlapping and interdependent systems of discrimination or disadvantage

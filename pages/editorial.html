Issue statement

Social Sciences Magazine for Students

Link it to art! Manet’s Olympia and intersectionality.

This issue brings together editorial writing, student news, critical reflection, narrative, and visual analysis to explore intersectionality as both a concept and a lived experience.

Contributors: Elise Crambes, Jaime Gu, Faidda Gure, and Max Lerner.

Preview

Tayo and Ryry are innocent childhood friends, but everything changes once they are enrolled in school. The story explores how social divisions are not simply seen on the surface, but filed away in records and written into history.

Two decades after Eduardo Bonilla-Silva’s foundational text, the revisiting essay returns to color-blind racism through a contemporary student lens, asking what has changed and what has not.
